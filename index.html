Abstract

We introduce a novel measure-theoretic framework for learning causal invariants in anti-causal domains. Our approach identifies causal invariants through direct measurement of product causal spaces, addressing covariate shift robustness through intervention-based causal abstraction. By decomposing representations into causal latent dynamics (Z_L) and causal latent abstraction (Z_H), we achieve state-of-the-art performance on challenging benchmarks while maintaining perfect environment independence.

Main Contributions

  • First measure-theoretic framework for anti-causal representation learning that handles both perfect and imperfect interventions
  • Two-level representation learning approach decomposing into causal latent dynamics and abstraction
  • Theoretical guarantees for kernel independence and optimization convergence
  • State-of-the-art performance on CMNIST with perfect environment independence
